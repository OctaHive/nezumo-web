## Nezumo is open to everyone

Meet **Nezumo** — an infinite canvas where teams think out loud. Bring in rough ideas, notes, and resources, then turn them together into diagrams, plans, and decisions worth sharing.

Ideas rarely arrive neatly organized. They are scattered across chats, documents, sticky notes, and your head. Nezumo gives everything a single place where you can capture it first and make sense of it together later.

This is more than another whiteboard. It is a shared space for modeling ideas: break a complex thought into parts, reveal connections, reshape the structure as you go, and carry a draft all the way to a result without switching between a dozen tools.

### From chaos to clarity

Start with a mess. Drop in thoughts, files, facts, questions, and alternatives. Then connect related elements, group what belongs together, rearrange the structure, and test different scenarios. Every move brings the bigger picture into focus.

The whole life of an idea stays in one place — from the first note and visual reference to the final diagram, plan, or presentation. There is no need to transfer, rebuild, or reformat your work.

### One canvas for the whole team

Invite teammates and work at the same time. Cursors, edits, and new ideas appear live, so everyone shares the same context.

- **Brainstorms** — capture ideas freely, then select the strongest ones
- **Research and analysis** — organize resources and spot patterns
- **Planning** — from task maps to a quarterly roadmap
- **Retrospectives** — review what worked and decide what changes next
- **Learning and onboarding** — explain with a living diagram instead of a static deck

### What's inside

- **Infinite canvas** with as much space as your thinking needs
- **Real-time collaboration** with live cursors and edits
- **Rich cards** for text, images, PDFs, video, audio, shapes, sticky notes, and tables
- **Projects and favorites** to keep growing work organized
- **Presentation mode** to share the result where it was created
- **Browser and desktop** access, including a macOS app

### How to start

Create your first board, invite a couple of teammates with a link, and begin with whatever feels messy. Nezumo keeps the tools close, saves your work automatically, and lets everyone see the whole picture.
